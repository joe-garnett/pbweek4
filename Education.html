Education By Computers

“We need technology in every classroom and in every student and teacher’s hand, because it is the pen and paper of our time, and it is the lens through which we experience much of our world.”

– David Warlick

Computers have revolutionised many areas of society, but none have been so drastically affected by their rise as education. Blackboards and chalk were necessary in classrooms before computers, and all notes were taken on paper and organised with physical folders, but virtual folders are arquably more common now.

Advantages of computers in education:

Slideshows

Slideshows have lots of information in an easy to understand and use format.

Online Resources

Teachers and student can both use online resources to find information instantly and do easy research into topics.

Interactive boards

Although whiteboards are often preffered, interactive boards allow for easier interaction with slides, etc

Teachers have had to adapt due to the radical changes affecting their industry ever since the introduction of computers, however it has proven to be all for the best as it makes teaching much easier and has introduced the concept of learning and teaching online, which was crucial during the times of the covid pandemic, and whenever in-person meetings aren't possible.

Conclusion

In conclusion, computers have drastically affected the way material is taught and has also propelled the globalisation of education and has made fields such as language much easier to teach and be taught.
